Ras Mohammed National Park

Egypt's premier national park offers dramatic drop-offs, massive gorgonian sea fans, and encounters with pelagic species. Shark Reef and Yolanda Reef are renowned for their staggering currents and nutrient-rich waters that attract schools of barracuda, snappers, and batfish.

Depth: 10m-40m+

Location: Sharm El Sheikh

Duration: 2 hours

Group Size: 2-4

Visibility: Moderate

The Giza plateau

Echoes of Eternity

The Giza plateau is home to the most iconic monuments of the ancient world. Constructed over 4,500 years ago, these immense structures were designed to protect the pharaohs' bodies and guide them into the afterlife. The precision of their construction remains a subject of awe and study to this day.

Best time to visit

October to April (Cooler Climate)

Discover Architectural Marvels

Khufu

The great pyramid

The oldest and largest of the three pyramids, originally standing at 146.6 meters. It was the tallest man-made structure in the world for over 3,800 years.

Khafre

The great sphinx

A mythical creature with the body of a lion and the head of a human, believed to represent Pharaoh Khafre. It stands as a timeless guardian of the plateau.

Menkaure

Pyramid of Menkaure

The smallest of the three main pyramids, noted for its complex internal chambers and the remains of its original red granite casing.
